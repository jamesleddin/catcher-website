How Your Data Flows

1. You connect your bank. Catcher redirects you to your bank's own login page via TrueLayer. We never see your username or password — your bank authenticates you directly.

2. Catcher reads your transactions. Read-only. We receive transaction descriptions, amounts, and dates. We cannot see your full account number or initiate any payments.

3. AI classifies your spending. Transactions are categorised by our AI (large language model technology). Your data is pseudonymised before analysis — no name, address, or account details are sent to the AI.

4. Letters are drafted for you. Negotiation letters are drafted using your transaction data. Sensitive personal information is never included unless you add it yourself before sending.

5. You're always in control. You review every letter before it's sent. You choose what to action. You can disconnect your bank and delete all data at any time — one tap, everything gone.

Your GDPR Rights — Exercisable In-App

FAQ
Questions you should ask
Is my bank data safe? ▾
Catcher connects via TrueLayer, an FCA-authorised Account Information Service Provider operating in Ireland under PSD2. We only have read-only access — we can never move money. We never see or store your login credentials. All data is encrypted with AES-256-GCM and stored in the EU.
Which Irish banks are supported? ▾
At launch: AIB, Bank of Ireland, Revolut, N26, and Permanent TSB. More banks and credit unions coming soon.
How do the AI letters work? ▾
When Catcher detects an overcharge, AI drafts a professional letter citing specific Irish regulators — FSPO for financial services, CRU for energy, ComReg for telecoms, CCPC for consumer rights. It includes competitor quotes when available and pre-fills the provider's email. You review before sending. Always your name, always your control.
Is Catcher a regulated financial service? ▾
No. Catcher is an information and advocacy tool, not a regulated financial advisor or credit intermediary. We don't provide financial advice, hold client money, or facilitate transactions. We provide tools and information to help you act on your own decisions. Your bank connection is provided by TrueLayer, which is regulated as an Account Information Service Provider.
When do I actually pay? ▾
Only after your bank data confirms a charge has dropped. Not when you send a letter. Not when a company responds. Only when real money is saved in your actual bank account. If Catcher doesn't save you money, you pay nothing.
Can I delete my data? ▾
Yes, at any time. One tap removes everything — your account, transactions, findings, and letters. We disconnect from your bank immediately and revoke all access tokens. GDPR Article 17 — your right to erasure.
How does flight compensation work? ▾
Catcher scans your bank transactions for airline purchases, then checks if those flights were delayed 3+ hours. Under EU Regulation 261/2004, you may be owed €250–€600 per passenger. Catcher drafts the claim letter with all flight details and legal references — no need for a claims company taking a large cut. Claims are generally valid for up to 6 years under Irish law.
Who sees my data? ▾
Your data is processed by five services, each with a signed Data Processing Agreement: TrueLayer (bank connection), Supabase (database, EU hosted), Anthropic (AI classification — your data is pseudonymised first), Stripe (payments), and Sentry (error monitoring — all personal data scrubbed). We never sell your data. We never share it with advertisers. We never will.
What happens if I disconnect? ▾
You can disconnect your bank at any time from the app. Catcher immediately revokes the TrueLayer access token — we lose all ability to read your transactions. Your existing findings and claims remain so you don't lose progress on active negotiations. To remove everything, tap "Delete my account" — all data is permanently erased within seconds.
